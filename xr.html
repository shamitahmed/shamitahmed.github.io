🐯Human Quadruped Locomotion for Exergames

    My Master’s thesis project introduces a novel human quadruped exergame where players control a virtual tiger through supine, bicycle-crunch-style movements tracked by a computer vision-based system. Designed for controller-free use in commercial activity parks, the system extends embodied interaction to non-human locomotion while effectively converging full-body exertion and gameplay immersion.



    Genre: Exergame, Platformer
    Role: Master´s Thesis Project
    Supervisor: Prof. Perttu Hämäläinen Gameplay

    Squat Games is a gestural interaction based exergame prototype I developed using Unity and Kinect.

    Player can embody a pogostick by controlling it with their gestures. Raising hands in specific direction changes the direction of the pogostick and squatting moves it forward.
